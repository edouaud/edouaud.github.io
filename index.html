What this job is about
Merval is a B2B SaaS provider that helps manufacturers in the retail industry improve the efficiency of their salesforce. Merval has been acquired by a new CEO, Loïc Brunaud in 2016. He brought a new vision and an up to date approche of his market. To help him completely rethink the products and services of the company, the management searched for a senior product manager / Chief Innovation Officer.

Since 2018, my roles are to:
  1. Study the market and survey customers,
  2. Suggest new products/features,
  3. Evalute the ratio between cost and benefit (RICE - Reach x Impact x Confidence / Effort),
  4. Prioritize the roadmap,
  5. Find the best tools, technologies, langages, system design,
  6. Create MVP if needed,
  7. Implement, develop (Agile/Scrum - 1 month sprint),
  8. Deploy and maintain the cloud (DevOps methodologies),
  9. Measure the onboarding and usage of these new features,
  10. Conclude from this data: keep, improve or remove,
  11. Start over :-)
WHO I WORK WITH
The CEO Loïc Brunaud hired me and interacts with me almost every day. Our products are used by salesforce of big companies amongh Colgate, Stanley Black & Decker, 3M, V33, Johnson & Johnson and many more.
Methodologies, Tools and Technologies I use
Product Management / Marker research: Prioritization methods (MoSCoW, RICE), Google Workplace, Trello, Notion.
User interviews: Google Forms for quantitative surveys and face-to-face interviews for qualitative surveys.
Data analysis: Big data storage: BigQuery; Business Intelligence: Google Looker Studio (formerly Data Studio).
Team management: Agile (scrum) with ClickUp, Google Workplace.
Technologies / DevOps: System design based on Google Cloud (Cloud Storage, Pub/Sub, Tables for AI, Cloud Functions, Compute Engine, BigQuery, Cloud SQL (Postgresql), Cloud Workstation). Langages and frameworks: TypeScript on nodeJS for the back-end, Javascript with Angular and Ionic for the front-end. Git.

What this project was about
I fulfilled a childhood dream of mixing aeronautics and computer science when I worked for 3 months on a startup project to create a Vertical Take Off and Landing Drone for 2 personnes to address the future on-demand urban air transportation:
  • Studied the market and already existing projects (CityAirbus, Lilium, Ehang, Volocopter, Cora, Uber Elevate, Sure Fly, etc).
